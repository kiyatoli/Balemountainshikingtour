Popular Trips in the Bale Mountains NP

The Bale Mountains are a great place to lace up your hiking boots and explore Ethiopia's great outdoors on foot. If you are basing yourself in the Bale Mountains and looking for more adventure, it's possible to arrange multi-day treks across the park. These treks range from 2-14 days with overnight stays at campsites scattered around the park. Additionally, horse riding is also possible in the national park.

You can modify the routes and the length of the treks to suit your fitness level. Generally, hiking in the Bale Mountains is relatively easy if you have average fitness.

Multi-Day Treks

The extraordinary, natural beauty and array of wildlife can be experienced by strapping on a good pair of boots and doing some trekking either on foot or on horseback. Introducing to the mountains, a multi-day trek is the best way to get the most out of the park. You can trek from camp to camp over a number of days, stopping at the best viewpoints along the way. Sleeping under the stars is a wonderful experience here, as the clear, pollution-free skies mean the night sky bursts with stars.

Camping in the Bale Mountains

Explore the stunning landscapes of the Bale Mountains.

Camping in the Bale Mountains is a fantastic way to explore the wilderness of the national park. The campsites of the Bale Mountains, while lacking in facilities, have beautiful views, and each has its own unique features. They are set between 17-22 km away from one another at 3000-4100 m.a.s.l., meaning that it takes less than a day's walk to travel from one to another.

Pro Tip: Bring warm clothing as temperatures can drop significantly at night.

Bale Mountains Camping

DINSHO HQ

For those planning to spend just one night in the Bale Mountains...

It's possible to camp at DINSHO, where the Bale Mountains National Park headquarters is located. Enjoy great views of wildlife like nyala, warthogs, and bushbucks. Tents, sleeping bags, and mattresses can be rented at the park entrance or arranged by our staff. For guaranteed quality, arranging these with us is the best option.

WEB VALLEY

Web Valley offers campsites near scenic high plateaus...

The majestic Ethiopian wolves can be spotted patrolling in the morning. Other wildlife includes spotted hyenas, golden jackals, olive baboons, and mountain nyalas. This area offers phenomenal bird life and allows for close encounters with the wolves.

WASEMA AND RAFU CAMPSITE

Hike to Mount Wasema for panoramic views...

The next day, trek across the park to reach the Stone Forest at RAFU Campsite, where the volcanic influence is evident in the surreal basalt pipes and granite boulders.

HARENNA FOREST

Harena Forest is Ethiopia's second-largest forest...

Home to native plants, mammals, and endemic bird species, this dense forest is famous for its unique biodiversity. The Gushrale Peak Trail offers panoramic views through mountain bamboo forests, with chances to spot the elusive Bale monkey.

COFFEE COLLECTOR

Manyete village is known for wild Arabica coffee...

The village in Harena Forest features massive trees, some over 30 meters tall, draped in epiphytes. Visit Sankete Coffee House to experience a traditional Ethiopian coffee ceremony while spotting local wildlife.
